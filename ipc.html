LAW SNIPPETS

Section 302

Section 302 of the IPC deals with the crime of murder. It defines murder as the intentional killing of a person with the knowledge that it is likely to cause death. Punishments can range from life imprisonment to the death penalty, depending on the circumstances.

Section 376

Section 376 addresses the crime of rape. It defines rape as non-consensual sexual intercourse with a person, and it includes various categories such as rape of a minor, rape within a marriage, and others. Penalties for rape can be severe, including imprisonment.

Section 420

Section 420 deals with the offense of cheating. It covers cases where a person dishonestly induces another person to deliver property, commit fraud, or deceive them. It is a punishable offense under this section.

Section 498A

Cruelty to Married Women: Section 498A is intended to protect married women from cruelty and harassment by their husbands or in-laws. It covers both physical and mental cruelty. It is a non-bailable offense, and those found guilty can face imprisonment.

Section 354

Assault or Criminal Force to Woman with Intent to Outrage Her Modesty: Section 354 deals with offenses involving the use of criminal force on a woman with the intent to outrage her modesty. This includes actions like molestation and harassment of women. Penalties may include imprisonment.

Section 377

Unnatural Offenses: Section 377 used to criminalize consensual homosexual acts; however, it was partially decriminalized in 2018. It remains on the books but is not applicable to consensual adult same-sex relationships.

Section 377A

Adultery: Section 497 of the IPC used to address the offense of adultery. However, it was struck down by the Supreme Court of India in 2018, decriminalizing adultery. Section 377A was related to the criminalization of adultery and was part of Section 497.

Section 511

Attempt to Commit an Offense: Section 511 of the IPC addresses attempts to commit offenses. It states that anyone who attempts to commit an offense, even if they do not succeed, can be punished with a penalty that may be less severe than the penalty for the actual offense but still proportional to the attempt..

Section 304B

Dowry Death: Section 304B deals with dowry deaths. It covers cases where the death of a woman occurs within seven years of her marriage and is related to harassment or cruelty for dowry. Those found guilty of dowry death can face significant penalties, including imprisonment.
